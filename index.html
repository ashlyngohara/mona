Twenty-one states have laws that explicitly prohibit discrimination on the basis of sexual orientation, but Missouri is not one of them.

The Missouri state legislature has introduced, and has failed to pass, the Missouri Nondiscrimination Act for twenty-one straight years. It was introduced again this year. House bill 1527, which is sponsored by Rep. Greg Razer, D-Kansas City, who is one of Missouri's two openly gay lawmakers.

The other is Rep. Tom Hannegan, R-St. Charles, who is sponsoring H.B. 1763, which also prohibits discrimination based on a person's sexual orientation or gender identity.

Data from the U.S. Census Bureau shows that the percentage of same-sex households in Missouri that are same-sex couples has more than doubled since 2009, from 28.9% (+/- 4.48%) to 63.3% (+/- 3.3). The Census Bureau distinguishes between households where the same-sex characteristic describes spouses and those where the householder is the same sex as an unmarried partner.
